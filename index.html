How to Develop Basic Journalism Skills

Assembly time: 30 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Do you want to learn more about writing in a journalistic style? Well, this may be the tutorial for you!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Notebook or Computer
  • Story Idea
  • Completed Interview

  1. Learn to write using the 5 W's

    Who, what, where, when, why and how. This is the foundation to every news story.

  2. Organize your story

    Choose which interview quotes you wish to use, begin writing the story using the inverted pyramid, think about a good lead for your story.

    Example of Inverted Pyramid structure.

  3. Begin writing your story using the inverted pyramid

    This is where the most important news appears at the top, followed by less crucial parts of the story. This is the format used for most news stories, so that the reader can get all the information they need to know despite not reading the entire article. It also helps in the event the article needs to be cut shorter.

  4. Add the lead to your story.

    Leads are typically one or two sentences long and are a crucial part of the story. This should summarize the entire article so the reader can decide if they want to continue reading or not.

  5. Use Quotes

    There are three different types of quotes you can use. The direct quote (someone’s exact words) is where you write word-for-word what your source has said, followed by a citation. A partial quote is where you use part of a direct quote. You use this when you want to include only a few specific words from your source. Lastly, a paraphrase is when you take your source's words and rewrite them in your own style.

  6. Edit Your Writing

    Make sure to edit your work. Keep in mind that you should use active voice, avoid run-on sentences, and format it correctly.
